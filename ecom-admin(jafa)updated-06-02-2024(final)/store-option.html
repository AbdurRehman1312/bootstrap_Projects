How to deduct bills of lading fees
What are bills of lading? It is a document issued by the shipping company to transport merchant orders to customers in exchange for a fee for this. The bill of lading includes details of the address of the sender and recipient, as well as details of the shipment, such as the contents of the cartons, their number, weight, etc.
What are the fees for bills of lading? These are fees charged by the shipping company for transporting shipments to customers. These fees depend on the contract concluded between the merchant and the shipping company based on the quantity of shipments sent. In Basket, we are interested in providing an easy experience and exclusive prices, as Basket is unique with fees specific to its trade and a distinct user experience that allows the merchant to activate the shipping companies he desires with the click of a button and sell directly without the need to communicate, negotiate and contract with shipping companies.
How to determine the customer's address
Select the appropriate method to obtain the customer's address on your store's checkout page:
  1. National Address: Customer selects city, neighborhood, street, zip code and house description (suitable for retail stores)
  2. Geographical location: The customer determines his location on the map (suitable for restaurants and immediate delivery)
  3. National address and geographical location: The customer specifies the city, neighborhood, street, zip code, description of the house, as well as its location on the map (suitable for stores that provide the option of immediate delivery and shipping)
The geographical location service is required by shipping companies in order for it to work properly
